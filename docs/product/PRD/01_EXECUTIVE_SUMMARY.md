# 1. Purpose

This document provides an executive overview of ATHENA.

It defines:

- Why ATHENA exists.
- The business objectives.
- The product objectives.
- The target users.
- The measurable success criteria.
- The implementation philosophy.

This document should be read by every engineer before contributing to ATHENA.

---

# 2. Executive Overview

ATHENA is an AI-assisted Decision Intelligence Platform designed for Indian financial markets.

Unlike conventional trading software, ATHENA focuses on improving **decision quality** rather than maximizing trade frequency.

The platform combines:

- Market Intelligence
- Quantitative Analysis
- Historical Probability
- Explainable Artificial Intelligence
- Risk Management
- Portfolio Intelligence
- Continuous Learning

ATHENA is designed to become progressively smarter through structured learning from completed decisions.

---

# 3. Product Vision

ATHENA exists to become the world's most trusted evidence-based decision intelligence platform.

The platform should help investors answer:

- Should I trade today?
- Why?
- How much should I risk?
- What is the probability of success?
- What could invalidate this decision?
- What can I learn afterwards?

---

# 4. Product Mission

The mission of ATHENA is:

> Improve investment decision quality through evidence, probability, disciplined risk management and continuous learning.

Success is measured by improving the quality of decisions—not by increasing trading activity.

# 7. Product Philosophy

ATHENA follows five core philosophies.

## Evidence

Every recommendation must be supported by measurable evidence.

---

## Probability

Recommendations are probability estimates—not predictions.

---

## Explainability

Every recommendation must explain:

- Why
- Why now
- Why this instrument
- Why this probability
- Why this risk

---

## Learning

Every completed trade contributes to future improvement.

---

## Human Control

AI assists.

Humans decide.

# 9. Product Positioning

ATHENA is positioned as a **Decision Intelligence Platform**.

It is **not**:

- a trading bot,
- a signal provider,
- an automated execution platform.

Instead, ATHENA is an intelligent research assistant that helps users make disciplined investment decisions.

---

# 10. Key Differentiators

ATHENA introduces capabilities that are uncommon in retail trading platforms.

## Decision Intelligence

Every recommendation includes reasoning, evidence and probability.

---

## Decision Replay

Every decision is archived with its complete context.

---

## Knowledge Engine

Every completed trade becomes research.

---

## Probability Intelligence

Historical patterns are used to estimate the likelihood of comparable outcomes.

---

## Trade DNA

ATHENA identifies recurring characteristics of successful and unsuccessful trades.

---

## Market Genome

ATHENA stores a structured fingerprint of market conditions for historical comparison.

---

## Strategy Lab

Every strategy is validated before production use.

---

# 11. Success Criteria

ATHENA succeeds when:

- Decision quality improves over time.
- Risk discipline is consistently maintained.
- Every recommendation is explainable.
- Users become more disciplined.
- Historical learning improves future recommendations.

---

# 12. Constraints

Version 1 intentionally excludes:

- Automated execution
- High-frequency trading
- Options strategies
- Crypto markets
- Forex markets
- International exchanges

These may be considered in future versions.

---

# 13. Risks

The project acknowledges the following risks.

- Market conditions evolve.
- Historical relationships may weaken.
- Poor data quality can affect outcomes.
- AI reasoning may be incomplete.
- Scope creep can delay delivery.

These risks will be managed through continuous validation and disciplined engineering.

---

# 14. Guiding Principles

Every future feature must improve at least one of:

- Decision Quality
- Risk Management
- Learning
- Research
- Transparency

Features that do not improve one of these dimensions should not be included.

---

# 15. Definition of Success

ATHENA will be considered successful when:

- Every decision is explainable.
- Every recommendation includes probability.
- Every trade contributes to learning.
- Capital preservation remains a core principle.
- Users consistently improve their investment decision-making process.
